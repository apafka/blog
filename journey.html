My Spiritual Journey: A Path Through Plant Medicines, Healing, and Enlightenment

Foreword: A Call to Share

Dear reader, this book is not just a collection of stories—it's a testament to the transformative power of plant medicines, the depths of personal healing, and the boundless potential of human energy. Inspired by a chance encounter with a wise elder during a simple taxi ride, I've decided to chronicle my experiences. These tales span nearly two decades of exploration, from accidental enlightenment to profound self-discovery, all woven together with the threads of spirituality, family, and growth. As we embark on this journey together, I invite you to reflect on your own path. May these words ignite something within you, as they have for me.

Chapter 1: The Spark of Inspiration

It all began with a client who reignited my passion for storytelling. I hadn't driven for Uber—or its equivalent—in about six months. One day, I decided to get back behind the wheel, simply to "put some work energy in motion." Little did I know how profoundly that intention would manifest.

My first passenger was an 80-year-old mathematician, crippled but sharp. He'd built critical software infrastructure for the Estonian government. As he struggled into the car, cursing under his breath—"Fuck, it’s difficult to get in"—I felt an immediate connection. Our 15-minute drive turned into a deep conversation. He shared his life stories, and I opened up about my adventures with plant medicines and shamanism. We vibed on a cosmic level, discussing God, oneness, and the interconnectedness of everything.

He urged me to record these experiences—for my children, their children, and humanity at large. As we parted at his destination, he said, "May God protect you." I was covered in goosebumps, a sensation I hadn't felt in ages. He mentioned his cousin worked for Elon Musk in Silicon Valley, adding an unexpected layer of synchronicity.

No new ride came immediately, so I pulled over and noticed an Audi with license plates ending in 940 driving away. Numbers like 4 and 9 have been following me—on neighbors' cars, around the city. It's a vibrational sign, a reminder of the energies at play. Energized, I sought out a book by Jaan Kaplinski, the author he'd recommended. That encounter was the catalyst for this book.

Chapter 2: The Essence of My Journey

Here I sit, writing in a cozy bar near my home in Laulasmaa, Estonia. The sea breeze whispers through the windows, and I realize I actually enjoy this spot. One day, I'll own land by the ocean, with a dream house for my family. But that's my vision—yours might be different, dear reader, or dear child of mine.

Why share this journey? Because mine could be one of history's greatest comebacks. My energy is unmatched, perhaps the first accumulation of its kind in human history. Arrogant? Maybe. But consider this: we're in an era of firsts, where ancient plant medicines from distant corners of the world—ayahuasca from the Amazon, peyote from the deserts, magic mushrooms from forests—are accessible together. No one before mixed them as I did, leading to accidental enlightenment.

It happened between 2018 and 2019 in southern Portugal. One night, I combined ayahuasca, peyote, magic mushrooms, and marijuana. Two nights prior, I'd added a microdose of LSD. During the ceremony, I was served rapé (Brazilian tobacco snuff). As I settled back, my consciousness surged beyond all prior experiences—pulled into a realm of pure light and oneness. I returned shaking, the room aglow. The shamans and 70 participants stared in awe. Two became one: non-duality realized.

Even in 2024, integration continues. I've become a husband to my strong-willed, loyal wife; a father to my clairvoyant daughter and my son, with whom I can now heal and learn from. I cherish them daily, grateful beyond words. This is just the start of my story, rooted in nearly 20 years with plant medicines. Without them, I might have spiraled into death, addiction, or despair. At 36, with my initiating shaman at 106, I have decades ahead to build, heal, and enjoy life with my family.

Chapter 3: Roots of the Path – My Background

I've started this project countless times, only to stall under high expectations. Now, it's a fun side endeavor with respect for its depth—no pressure, just flow.

I was an only child, born to a Russian gangster father and a broken Estonian mother. I felt unwanted, a burden. My dad mistreated my mom, leaving her as a single parent after his betrayals. Showered with material gifts but starved of love, I grew up confused. Dad's bad decisions led to financial ruin; Mom worked tirelessly to recover. She'd been hurt by men, so warmth was scarce. Through healing, I understood: she couldn't give what she never received. Still, she provided education and shelter.

Gifting her grandchildren has brought her joy, though it may take time for her to fully see it. Separated from my ungrounded father—traumatized by his own father's early death and a harsh mother—we drifted from roots into toxic vibes. Dad drank himself to death, betrayed by "friends." I had fake ones too, bouncing between schools, directionless. Mom pushed me through university; I lacked the courage to refuse.

This chaos led me to plant medicines—a rabbit hole at first, until Portugal. There, I realized my energy surpassed others'. I hold the line, for my future and my family's. The stories ahead reveal how.

Chapter 7: Global Quests – From Rainforest to Mongolia

My leap year of travel: Sri Lanka, Koh Phangan (Thailand), Bali, Portugal, Mallorca, Eagle Condor Festival in Brazil's Alto Paraiso, then three months with the Yawanawá in Acre's rainforest.

I know leaders like Biraci, Nawashahu, and Peu Yawanawá, but their path isn't mine anymore—perhaps family trips later.

Ceremonies, singing, truth-seeking filled days. A one-month dieta ended in unease; energies clashed. I fled, affirming self-treatment as kingly.

In a day-long ayahuasca session (eight cups), I journeyed deep, craving a hug—learning self-love. This echoed Thailand's Sanctuary resort, where pacing a yoga hall bloomed a heart-seed of self-love, unprompted by psychedelics.

In Nova Esperança village, hammock-sleeping in the chief's house, a spirit visited post-Chinggis Khan podcast—omnipresent, unafraid.

Post-dieta, Europe return: swollen leg in Porto from post-rainforest cookies. Unhappy in Estonia, I pursued Mongolia's Burkhan Khaldun.

Visa in Stockholm, loan-funded tickets. Hostel in Ulaanbaatar, emails to the president for access (diplomats only). Found a Russian-speaking driver; 1.5 days to the base.

Climbed on my birthday (27/28?), with mushrooms, cannabis cookies, fasting. Alone, the mountain embraced me—wolves, bears, deer in the national park. Nomads dotted vast lands.

Ascending, winds circled, spirits connected. Atop, an angel figure: "You made it; live on your terms." Descending, phantom horsemen—Chinggis Khan's army—chased me out.
